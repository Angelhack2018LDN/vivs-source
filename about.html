How it works

Before a disaster

In known high risk areas, families are recorded. Data capture is done by:

  • photography (image recognition)
  • basic relevant data capture.
  • a secret question and answer known to the family
  • Data is written to block chain.

After a disaster

Scenario 1: a parent looking for a missing child
  1. Sends an SMS or MMS to Found Already looking for child.
  2. Chatbot (IBM Watson) sends reply back, asking for more information if required.
  3. Information is checked against database for missing children and known data of families.
  4. SMS indicates parent’s location
  5. Chatbot send a location (eg. refugee camp) to the parent to go to, either where the child’s likely located, or nearest NGO base where further data can be captured.
Scenario 2: a child is found in distress
  1. Child is taken to a safe location (eg. refugee camp), registered and interviewed.
  2. Details are searched against the blockchain data.
  3. We then either know who their parents are, or have to find the parent.

Benefits

In the aftermath of a natural disaster the speed of reuniting children with their families is crucial to the safety, mental and physical wellbeing of the children.

Found Already uses smart technology to help speed up the reunification time and ensures every child is matched with the correct guardian.

Why it’s better than what they do now...

Example of a current solution (Save the Children in South Sudan): ‘Active tracing’ (physical on-location search for parents) was more effective than passive ‘Electronic data matching’ a factor of 12.

Our solution leverages this further by registering the data before a disaster happens. This data capture can be carried out in conjunction with other humanitarian projects in known risk area.
